This function builds the strain localisation tensor of a general ellipsoid embedded in an ANISOTROPIC matrix. The localisation tensor \(A\) is defined as follows :

\[\epsilon = A:E_0\]

where \(E_0\) is the uniform strain tensor imposed at infinity, and \(\epsilon\) is the strain tensor solution of Eshelby problem for the ellipsoid. The ellipsoid also has a specific orientation given by the vectors \(n_a\), \(n_b\). A LOCAL basis can then be defined as n_a, n_b, n_c where n_c=cross_product(n_a,n_b)

Returns
an object of type st2tost2<3u,real>, which is the fourth-order localisation tensor \(A\) in the GLOBAL BASIS
