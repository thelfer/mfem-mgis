◆ VoigtIndex()

template<unsigned short N>
TFEL_HOST_DEVICE constexpr unsigned short tfel::math::VoigtIndex ( const unsigned short  i,
const unsigned short  j 
)
constexpr

This function returns the Voigt index of two indices (beginning from 0). Hence, the correspondence is, in 3d:

\[(0,0)->0, (1,1)->1, (2,2)->2, (0,1) or (1,0) -> 3, (0,2) or (2,0) -> 4, (1,2) or (2,1)-> 5\]

. In 2d, it is

\[(0,0)->0, (1,1)->1, (2,2)->2, (0,1) or (1,0) -> 3\]

. In 1d,

\[(0,0)->0, (1,1)->1, (2,2)->2\]

. Be careful to the Voigt indices between 3 and 5, which are different from the usual convention:

\[\varepsilon_{12}\]

is usually the last component of the vector

\[\varepsilon\]

in the usual Voigt notation, but here it is situated as the fourth component, in order that the vector in dimension 2 is just a truncation of the vector in dimension 3.

an unsigned short
